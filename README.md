# Tackling Malnutrition in Malaysia from Root to Relief (2015–2022)

## 📌 1. Project Overview
Despite Malaysia's steady economic growth, food insecurity and "hidden hunger" remain critical challenges affecting low-income families (B40), urban poor, and rural/indigenous communities. 

This Data Analytics & Power BI project investigates the root causes and socioeconomic impacts of malnutrition in Malaysia from 2015 to 2022. By analyzing datasets from the **Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM)** and the **World Health Organization (WHO)**, this project examines food price inflation, regional GDP disparities, child stunting prevalence, and labor productivity declines across key economic sectors.

## 📊 3. Key Findings & Analytics Breakdown

### Section I: Economic Causes & Food Inflation Analysis (CPI)
* **B40 Household Expenditure:** Low-income households spend **35% of their total budget on food** and **30% on housing**, making them extremely vulnerable to inflation spikes.
* **Urban vs. Rural CPI Trend:** Between 2015 and 2022, the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for *Food & Non-Alcoholic Beverages* rose sharply, with urban areas experiencing higher price inflation than rural areas post-2020.
* **Regional Disparities:** States with lower GDP contributions (e.g., Kelantan, Perlis, W.P. Labuan) face structural constraints and higher nutritional vulnerability compared to economic hubs like Selangor and W.P. Kuala Lumpur.

### Section II: Societal Impact – Child Stunting Trends
* **Prevalence Among Children (<5 Years):** Based on WHO model estimates, child stunting prevalence in Malaysia rose from **19.5% in 2015 to 23.0% in 2022**.
* **Gender Disparity:** Male children consistently showed higher stunting rates (24.3% in 2022) compared to female children (21.6% in 2022).
* **Total Affected Population:** By 2022, an estimated **541,900 children** under age 5 in Malaysia were affected by stunting.

### Section III: Labor Productivity & Economic Consequences
* **Labor-Intensive Sectors:** Physically demanding sectors like **Construction** and **Agriculture** suffered severe drops in output per hour worked between 2019 and 2021 (e.g., ~55% drop in hourly productivity in Construction).
* **Control Comparison:** Capital-intensive sectors like **Finance & Insurance** maintained stable output during the same period, proving that nutritional stress and physical fatigue disproportionately affect physical workforces.

## 💡 5. Strategic Recommendations & Relief Framework
1. **Targeted Subsidies:** Direct price support for essential food groups tailored specifically for B40 households.
2. **Community-Driven Initiatives:** Scaling up food bank models (such as *Kechara Soup Kitchen*) to bridge emergency relief gaps in high-density urban poor areas.
3. **Data-Driven Intervention:** Utilizing state-level CPI and stunting data to deploy medical and nutritional outreach teams directly to high-priority regions.
